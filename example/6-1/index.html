Essay

We live in a busy world. While most of the time we seem to be able to cope with a fast pace and seemingly endless jobs, we might not see our pressure is pilling up along the way. When we can sense the pressure, it might gave already grown too much as many different things have contributed to the problem. The secret to happiness is thus to understand why we are unhappy, which is, suprisingly, a difficult task.

When our pressure increases, many of us could choose to avoid directly dealing with the pressure, because it is easier to distract yourself from the pressure by, for example, having some fun. However, when the fun comes to an end, the pressure and the associated negative emotion would hit you agian very quickly - it is never successful to try to forget your pressure. To make your life happy again, we really have to get to the bottom of the problem to truly solve it.

Figuring out the underlying "causes" of the problem is, ironically, the most challenging part. Note that we are talking about "causes", not just "a cause". We might be able to identify some potential factors such as the unfinished works of your job, your arguments with your significant other, loud noises surrounding your place, or just some sleep issues. But we need to know which ones truly bother us. Maybe we ahve not heard of loud noises recently, or we have already fixed our relationship with our significant other. Gradually, we can narrow down to real culprits.
